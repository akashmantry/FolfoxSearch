<p>The <b>1928 National Convention</b> of the <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican Party</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> was held at <a href="/wiki/Convention_Hall" title="Convention Hall">Convention Hall</a> in <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">Missouri</a>, from June 12 to June 15, 1928.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>
<p>Because <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President</a> <a href="/wiki/Calvin_Coolidge" title="Calvin Coolidge">Coolidge</a> had announced unexpectedly he would not run for re-election in 1928, <a href="/wiki/United_States_Secretary_of_Commerce" title="United States Secretary of Commerce">Commerce Secretary</a> <a href="/wiki/Herbert_Hoover" title="Herbert Hoover">Herbert Clark Hoover</a> became the natural front-runner for the Republican nomination. Former <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">Illinois</a> <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Illinois" title="Governor of Illinois">Governor</a> <a href="/wiki/Frank_Lowden" class="mw-redirect" title="Frank Lowden">Frank Lowden</a> and <a href="/wiki/Kansas" title="Kansas">Kansas</a> <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">Senator</a> <a href="/wiki/Charles_Curtis" title="Charles Curtis">Charles Curtis</a> were candidates for the nomination but stood no chance against the popular and accomplished Hoover. <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">Chicago</a> <a href="/wiki/Mayor_of_Chicago" title="Mayor of Chicago">Mayor</a> <a href="/wiki/William_Hale_Thompson" title="William Hale Thompson">William Hale Thompson</a> considered himself a candidate, but without the support of <a href="/wiki/Ruth_Hanna_McCormick" title="Ruth Hanna McCormick">Ruth Hanna McCormick</a>, his candidacy was unsuccessful.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Hoover was nominated on the first <a href="/wiki/Ballot" title="Ballot">ballot</a> with 837 votes to 72 for Lowden and 64 for Curtis and the rest scattered. <a href="/w/index.php?title=John_L._McNab&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="John L. McNab (page does not exist)">John L. McNab</a> delivered Hoover's nomination address. In his acceptance speech he said that "We in <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">America</a> today are nearer to the final triumph over <a href="/wiki/Poverty" title="Poverty">poverty</a> ever before in the history of any land"; this and other optimistic remarks about the country's future were held against him in the <a href="/wiki/U.S._presidential_election,_1932" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. presidential election, 1932">1932 election</a>, which he lost to <a href="/wiki/Franklin_D._Roosevelt" title="Franklin D. Roosevelt">Franklin D. Roosevelt</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Platform">Platform</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1928_Republican_National_Convention&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Platform">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Political_platform" class="mw-redirect" title="Political platform">platform</a> praised the Coolidge administration for the prosperity of the mid-1920s, and also promised reduction of the <a href="/wiki/National_debt" class="mw-redirect" title="National debt">national debt</a>, tax reduction, retention of the protective tariff, opposition of cancellation of foreign debts, settlement of claims from <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> from foreign governments, continuation of the Coolidge foreign policy, support of arbitration treaties, civil service protection, a tariff for <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">agricultural</a> protection and continued farm exports, aid to the coal-mining industry, continued appropriations for <a href="/wiki/Highway" title="Highway">highway</a> construction, the right to <a href="/wiki/Collective_bargaining" title="Collective bargaining">collective bargaining</a>, regulation of railroads, a continued independent American merchant marine, government supervision of radio facilities, construction of <a href="/wiki/Waterway" title="Waterway">waterways</a> to help transportation of bulk goods, support for <a href="/wiki/War_veteran" class="mw-redirect" title="War veteran">war veterans</a>, federal regulation of <a href="/wiki/Public_utilities" class="mw-redirect" title="Public utilities">public utilities</a>, conservation, vigorous law enforcement, honest government, continued reclamation of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Arid_land&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Arid land (page does not exist)">arid lands</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Western_United_States" title="Western United States">West</a>, improvement of air-mail service, restricted <a href="/wiki/Immigration" title="Immigration">immigration</a> and <a href="/wiki/Naturalization" title="Naturalization">naturalization</a> of foreign immigrants in America, continued enforcement of the <a href="/wiki/Washington_Naval_Treaty" title="Washington Naval Treaty">Washington Naval Treaty</a>, continued status of territory status for <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">Alaska</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hawaii" title="Hawaii">Hawaii</a> and called for more women in public service, right of the President to draft defense material resources and services, creation of an <a href="/wiki/Indian_Commission" class="mw-redirect" title="Indian Commission">Indian Commission</a>, an <a href="/w/index.php?title=Anti-Lynching_Law&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Anti-Lynching Law (page does not exist)">Anti-Lynching Law</a> and promised continued Home-Rule for the American Citizen.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Candidates_for_the_nomination_before_and_during_the_convention">Candidates for the nomination before and during the convention</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1928_Republican_National_Convention&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Candidates for the nomination before and during the convention">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After Coolidge announced that he would not run for a second full term in August 1927, Herbert Hoover emerged as the front-runner. Illinois Governor Frank Lowden, Vice President <a href="/wiki/Charles_Dawes" class="mw-redirect" title="Charles Dawes">Charles Dawes</a>, and Senators <a href="/wiki/James_Eli_Watson" title="James Eli Watson">James Eli Watson</a> of Indiana, <a href="/wiki/Charles_Curtis" title="Charles Curtis">Charles Curtis</a> of Kansas, <a href="/wiki/Guy_D._Goff" title="Guy D. Goff">Guy D. Goff</a> of West Virginia, and <a href="/wiki/Frank_B._Willis" title="Frank B. Willis">Frank Willis</a> of Ohio also loomed as potential challengers to Hoover. Hoover had won respect among many for his work in the Wilson, Harding, and Coolidge administrations, but many party regulars distrusted his loyalty to the party on issues of both policy and patronage. Some progressive Republicans, such as California Senator <a href="/wiki/Hiram_Johnson" title="Hiram Johnson">Hiram Johnson</a>, also disliked Hoover, although others such as Senator <a href="/wiki/William_E._Borah" class="mw-redirect" title="William E. Borah">William E. Borah</a> favored Hoover's candidacy. Many party leaders pressured Secretary of the Treasury <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Mellon" title="Andrew Mellon">Andrew Mellon</a> to run, but at 73 years old in 1928, Mellon felt that he was too old to run. Mellon sought to convince former Secretary of State and 1916 Republican nominee <a href="/wiki/Charles_Evan_Hughes" class="mw-redirect" title="Charles Evan Hughes">Charles Evan Hughes</a> to run, but Hughes refused to campaign for the nomination.<sup id="cite_ref-rusnak_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rusnak-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>With no strong alternative, Hoover appeared to be the likely nominee at the start of the convention, but many in the party still opposed his candidacy. A "draft Coolidge" movement emerged, but the movement collapsed as it became clear that Coolidge would not run again.<sup id="cite_ref-oulahan_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oulahan-4">[4]</a></sup> With Coolidge out of the running, Senate Majority Leader <a href="/wiki/Charles_Curtis" title="Charles Curtis">Charles Curtis</a> tried to rally Hoover's rivals around his own candidacy.<sup id="cite_ref-oulahan_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oulahan-4">[4]</a></sup> However, Hoover won the nomination on the first ballot.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Candidates_for_the_vice_presidential_nomination">Candidates for the vice presidential nomination</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1928_Republican_National_Convention&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Candidates for the vice presidential nomination">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>At the start of the convention, Vice President <a href="/wiki/Charles_G._Dawes" title="Charles G. Dawes">Charles G. Dawes</a>, former Kansas Governor <a href="/wiki/Henry_Justin_Allen" title="Henry Justin Allen">Henry Justin Allen</a>, New Jersey Senator <a href="/wiki/Walter_Edge" class="mw-redirect" title="Walter Edge">Walter Edge</a>, New Hampshire Senator <a href="/wiki/George_H._Moses" title="George H. Moses">George H. Moses</a>, Connecticut Congressman <a href="/wiki/John_Q._Tilson" title="John Q. Tilson">John Q. Tilson</a>, and Ambassador <a href="/wiki/Alanson_B._Houghton" title="Alanson B. Houghton">Alanson B. Houghton</a> were mentioned as potential running mates for Hoover.<sup id="cite_ref-oulahan_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oulahan-4">[4]</a></sup> Kansas Senator <a href="/wiki/Charles_Curtis" title="Charles Curtis">Charles Curtis</a> was also mentioned as a possibility, but Curtis was not yet ready to concede the presidential nomination to Hoover.<sup id="cite_ref-oulahan_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oulahan-4">[4]</a></sup> After Hoover won the presidential nomination, Moses, Illinois Senator <a href="/wiki/Charles_S._Deneen" title="Charles S. Deneen">Charles S. Deneen</a>, and Massachusetts Governor <a href="/wiki/Channing_Cox" class="mw-redirect" title="Channing Cox">Channing Cox</a> were named as the most likely vice presidential nominees, with a re-nomination for Dawes also a possibility.<sup id="cite_ref-oulahan2_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-oulahan2-5">[5]</a></sup> Party leaders considered nominating Cox or Dawes, but the former was vetoed by Utah Senator <a href="/wiki/Reed_Smoot" title="Reed Smoot">Reed Smoot</a>, and the latter's support of <a href="/wiki/McNary-Haugen" class="mw-redirect" title="McNary-Haugen">McNary-Haugen</a> made him unacceptable to Hoover and Coolidge.<sup id="cite_ref-clements_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-clements-6">[6]</a></sup> Curtis, possibly with the support of Coolidge, was nominated by the party leaders, and the convention ratified the choice.<sup id="cite_ref-clements_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-clements-6">[6]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Prayers">Prayers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1928_Republican_National_Convention&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Prayers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Each of the four days of the convention opened with a lengthy <a href="/wiki/Invocation" title="Invocation">invocation</a> by a different clergymen—one <a href="/wiki/Judaism" title="Judaism">Jewish</a>, one <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Church" class="mw-redirect" title="Roman Catholic Church">Catholic</a>, one <a href="/wiki/Episcopal_Church_(United_States)" title="Episcopal Church (United States)">Episcopalian</a>, one <a href="/wiki/Methodist" class="mw-redirect" title="Methodist">Methodist</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Together, these four religious groups formed a majority of Americans at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>
<p>All of the clergy were based in Missouri, where the convention was held. Each was listed among the convention officers as an official <a href="/wiki/Chaplain" title="Chaplain">chaplain</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On June 12, the opening prayer was given by Bishop <a href="/wiki/Sidney_Catlin_Partridge" title="Sidney Catlin Partridge">S. C. Partridge</a> of the Episcopal Diocese of West Missouri.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> Speakers on the second through fourth days were Catholic Bishop <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Francis_Lillis" title="Thomas Francis Lillis">Thomas F. Lillis</a> of the Diocese of Kansas City,<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup> Rabbi <a href="/w/index.php?title=Herman_M._Cohen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Herman M. Cohen (page does not exist)">Herman M. Cohen</a> of Congregation Keneseth Israel-Beth Sholom, Kansas City,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> and Bishop <a href="/wiki/Ernest_Lynn_Waldorf" title="Ernest Lynn Waldorf">E. L. Waldorf</a> of the Methodist Episcopal Diocese of Kansas City.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
