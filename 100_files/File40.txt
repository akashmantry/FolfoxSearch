<p>The <b>2017 Masters Tournament</b> was the 81st edition of the <a href="/wiki/Masters_Tournament" title="Masters Tournament">Masters Tournament</a> and the first of golf's four <a href="/wiki/Men%27s_major_golf_championships" title="Men's major golf championships">major championships</a> in 2017. It was held on <span class="nowrap">April 6–9</span> at <a href="/wiki/Augusta_National_Golf_Club" title="Augusta National Golf Club">Augusta National Golf Club</a> in <a href="/wiki/Augusta,_Georgia" title="Augusta, Georgia">Augusta, Georgia</a>.</p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sergio_Garc%C3%ADa" title="Sergio García">Sergio García</a> beat <a href="/wiki/Justin_Rose" title="Justin Rose">Justin Rose</a> in a sudden-death playoff after they both completed the 72 holes in nine-under-par. This was García's first major championship title, which came in his 74th attempt. Previously, García finished in the top-10 on 22 occasions. He was the first Spaniard to win the Masters since <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mar%C3%ADa_Olaz%C3%A1bal" title="José María Olazábal">José María Olazábal</a> in <a href="/wiki/1999_Masters_Tournament" title="1999 Masters Tournament">1999</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Field">Field</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Masters_Tournament&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Field">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Masters has the smallest field of the four major championships. Officially, the Masters remains an invitation event, but there is a set of qualifying criteria that determines who is included in the field. Each player is classified according to the first category by which he qualified, with other categories in which he qualified shown in parentheses.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p>Golfers who qualify based solely on their performance in amateur tournaments (categories 7–11) must remain amateurs on the starting day of the tournament to be eligible to play.</p>

<p><i>Wednesday, April 5, 2017</i></p>
<p>Heavy rain forced the cancellation of the <a href="/wiki/Masters_Tournament_Par-3_contest" title="Masters Tournament Par-3 contest">Par-3 contest</a> for the first time in its history. <a href="/wiki/Mike_Weir" title="Mike Weir">Mike Weir</a> recorded the only hole-in-one before play was suspended.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Second_round">Second round</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Masters_Tournament&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Second round">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Friday, April 7, 2017</i></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/Charley_Hoffman" title="Charley Hoffman">Charley Hoffman</a> fell back to the pack with a three-over round of 75 and into a four-way tie for the lead. <a href="/wiki/Rickie_Fowler" title="Rickie Fowler">Rickie Fowler</a> had four birdies and an eagle on his way to a round of 67 (−5), the lowest score of the round, and tied for the lead along with <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Garc%C3%ADa" title="Sergio García">Sergio García</a> and <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Pieters" title="Thomas Pieters">Thomas Pieters</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup> Garcia was originally credited with a triple-bogey seven on the 10th, but his score was later corrected to a five. Fifteen players were within five shots of the lead, including past champions <a href="/wiki/Fred_Couples" title="Fred Couples">Fred Couples</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phil_Mickelson" title="Phil Mickelson">Phil Mickelson</a>, <a href="/wiki/Adam_Scott_(golfer)" title="Adam Scott (golfer)">Adam Scott</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Spieth" title="Jordan Spieth">Jordan Spieth</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup> Amateur <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Hagestad" title="Stewart Hagestad">Stewart Hagestad</a> became the first <a href="/wiki/U.S._Mid-Amateur" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Mid-Amateur">U.S. Mid-Amateur</a> champion to make the cut at the Masters since the winner of that tournament was granted entry in 1989.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Final_round">Final round</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Masters_Tournament&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Final round">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Sunday, April 9, 2017</i></p>
<p>After <a href="/wiki/Sergio_Garc%C3%ADa" title="Sergio García">Sergio García</a> birdied two of his first three holes he opened up a three-shot lead. Beginning at the 6th, however, <a href="/wiki/Justin_Rose" title="Justin Rose">Justin Rose</a> recorded three consecutive birdies to tie García. With bogeys by García at the 10th and 11th, Rose went ahead by two shots.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> At the 13th, García was forced to take a drop when his tee shot found the trees, but was able to get <a href="/wiki/Glossary_of_golf#U" title="Glossary of golf">up and down</a> to save par while Rose missed his birdie attempt.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> García made birdie at the 14th to get within one and hit his approach to the par-5 15th to 14 feet. After converting the eagle attempt, he once again tied Rose, who made birdie. On the par-3 16th, both hit approaches to within 8 feet, and Rose made his birdie while García missed. At the 17th, however, Rose found the greenside bunker and suffered a bogey while Garcia two-putted for par, once again tying for the lead heading to the last hole. Rose missed a seven-footer for birdie, while García missed a five-foot putt to win the championship, forcing a sudden-death playoff. <a href="/wiki/Charl_Schwartzel" title="Charl Schwartzel">Charl Schwartzel</a>, the <a href="/wiki/2011_Masters_Tournament" title="2011 Masters Tournament">2011</a> champion, recorded four birdies on the back-nine to shoot 68 (−4) and finish in third place, three shots behind García and Rose.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Pieters" title="Thomas Pieters">Thomas Pieters</a> also shot 68 after making four straight birdies on holes 12–15 and finished in a tie for fourth place. <a href="/wiki/Matt_Kuchar" title="Matt Kuchar">Matt Kuchar</a> birdied three consecutive holes on his final nine, then made a hole-in-one at the 16th to equal the lowest score of the round with 67 (−5) and finish in a tie with Pieters. <a href="/wiki/Rickie_Fowler" title="Rickie Fowler">Rickie Fowler</a> began the round a shot out of the lead but recorded a score of 76 (+4) and dropped to 11th, while <a href="/wiki/2015_Masters_Tournament" title="2015 Masters Tournament">2015</a> champion <a href="/wiki/Jordan_Spieth" title="Jordan Spieth">Jordan Spieth</a>, two back at the start of the round, shot 75 (+3).<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup></p>
<p>After García took his drop on 13, some TV viewers reported the possibility that he caused his ball to move while removing some pine straw near his ball. Prior to the conclusion of the round Masters Officials determined there was no penalty.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup> Per Rule 18-2 (Decision 18/4) even if high definition TV camera evidence shows movement, there is no penalty if it is deemed that the movement was not reasonably discernible to the naked eye at the time.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[19]</a></sup></p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Playoff">Playoff</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2017_Masters_Tournament&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Playoff">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Beginning the sudden-death playoff at the par-4 18th, Rose's drive found the trees and he was forced to chip out. García's drive found the fairway and he hit his approach to 12 feet, while Rose was 14 feet away for par.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">[20]</a></sup> Rose missed the putt, giving García two putts to win the championship, but he converted the birdie to win his first career major championship. The win came in García's 19th Masters appearance and 74th major championship, the most by any player before their first win.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">[21]</a></sup></p>
