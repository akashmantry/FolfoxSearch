<p><b>2012 (<a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MMXII</a>)</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Leap_year" title="Leap year">leap year</a> <a href="/wiki/Leap_year_starting_on_Sunday" title="Leap year starting on Sunday">starting on Sunday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> AG) of the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>, the 2012th year of the <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">Common Era</a> (CE) and <i><a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a></i> (AD) designations, the 12th year of the <a href="/wiki/3rd_millennium" title="3rd millennium">3rd&#160;millennium</a>, the 12th year of the <a href="/wiki/21st_century" title="21st century">21st&#160;century</a>, and the 3rd year of the <a href="/wiki/2010s" title="2010s">2010s</a> decade.</p>
<p>2012 was designated as:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/International_Year_of_Cooperatives" title="International Year of Cooperatives">International Year of Cooperatives</a><sup id="cite_ref-2012_-_International_Year_of_Cooperatives_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2012_-_International_Year_of_Cooperatives-1">[1]</a></sup></li>
<li>International Year of Sustainable Energy for All<sup id="cite_ref-2012_-_International_Year_of_Sustainable_Energy_for_All_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2012_-_International_Year_of_Sustainable_Energy_for_All-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="January">January</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: January">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_23" title="January 23">January 23</a> – <a href="/wiki/Iran%E2%80%93European_Union_relations" title="Iran–European Union relations">Iran–European Union relations</a>: The <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a> adopts an <a href="/wiki/Embargo" title="Embargo">embargo</a> against <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> in protest of its continued effort to enrich <a href="/wiki/Uranium" title="Uranium">uranium</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="February">February</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: February">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_6" title="February 6">February 6</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Diamond_Jubilee_of_Elizabeth_II" title="Diamond Jubilee of Elizabeth II">Diamond Jubilee of Queen Elizabeth II</a> marks the 60th anniversary of her accession to the thrones of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, and the 60th anniversary of her becoming <a href="/wiki/Head_of_the_Commonwealth" title="Head of the Commonwealth">Head of the Commonwealth</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_19" title="February 19">February 19</a> – <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">Iran</a> suspends oil exports to Britain and France, following sanctions put in place by the European Union and the United States in January.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_21" title="February 21">February 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/Greek_government-debt_crisis" title="Greek government-debt crisis">Greek government-debt crisis</a>: <a href="/wiki/Eurozone" title="Eurozone">Eurozone</a> finance ministers reach an agreement on a second, <a href="/wiki/Euro" title="Euro">€</a>130-billion Greek <a href="/wiki/Bailout" title="Bailout">bailout</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="March">March</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: March">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_13" title="March 13">March 13</a> – After 244 years since its first publication, the <i><a href="/wiki/Encyclop%C3%A6dia_Britannica" title="Encyclopædia Britannica">Encyclopædia Britannica</a></i> discontinues its print edition.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[8]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_22" title="March 22">March 22</a> – The <a href="/wiki/List_of_heads_of_state_of_Mali" title="List of heads of state of Mali">President of Mali</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amadou_Toumani_Tour%C3%A9" title="Amadou Toumani Touré">Amadou Toumani Touré</a>, is ousted in a <a href="/wiki/2012_Malian_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="2012 Malian coup d'état">coup d'état</a> after mutinous soldiers attack government offices.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[9]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="April">April</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: April">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_6" title="April 6">April 6</a> – The <a href="/wiki/National_Movement_for_the_Liberation_of_Azawad" title="National Movement for the Liberation of Azawad">National Movement for the Liberation of Azawad</a> unilaterally declares the independence of <a href="/wiki/Azawad" title="Azawad">Azawad</a> from <a href="/wiki/Mali" title="Mali">Mali</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_12" title="April 12">April 12</a> – <a href="/wiki/Mutiny" title="Mutiny">Mutinous</a> soldiers in <a href="/wiki/Guinea-Bissau" title="Guinea-Bissau">Guinea-Bissau</a> stage a <a href="/wiki/2012_Guinea-Bissau_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat" title="2012 Guinea-Bissau coup d'état">coup d'état</a> and take control of the capital city, <a href="/wiki/Bissau" title="Bissau">Bissau</a>. They arrest interim President <a href="/wiki/Raimundo_Pereira" title="Raimundo Pereira">Raimundo Pereira</a> and leading presidential candidate <a href="/wiki/Carlos_Gomes_J%C3%BAnior" title="Carlos Gomes Júnior">Carlos Gomes Júnior</a> in the midst of a <a href="/wiki/Guinea-Bissau_presidential_election,_2012" title="Guinea-Bissau presidential election, 2012">presidential election</a> campaign.<sup id="cite_ref-ap_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ap-11">[11]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_13" title="April 13">April 13</a> – <a href="/wiki/Kwangmy%C5%8Fngs%C5%8Fng-3" title="Kwangmyŏngsŏng-3">Kwangmyŏngsŏng-3</a>, a <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">North Korean</a> <a href="/wiki/Earth_observation_satellite" title="Earth observation satellite">Earth observation satellite</a>, explodes shortly after launch. The United States and other countries had called the impending launch a violation of <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council" title="United Nations Security Council">United Nations Security Council</a> demands.<sup id="cite_ref-NK_satellite_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NK_satellite-12">[12]</a></sup> The launch was planned to mark the centenary of the birth of <a href="/wiki/Kim_Il-sung" title="Kim Il-sung">Kim Il-sung</a>, the founder of the republic.<sup id="cite_ref-NK_satellite_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NK_satellite-12">[12]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/April_26" title="April 26">April 26</a> – Former <a href="/wiki/Liberia" title="Liberia">Liberian</a> President <a href="/wiki/Charles_Taylor_(Liberian_politician)" title="Charles Taylor (Liberian politician)">Charles Taylor</a> is found guilty on 11 counts of aiding and abetting war crimes and crimes against humanity during the <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Leone_Civil_War" title="Sierra Leone Civil War">Sierra Leone Civil War</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="May">May</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=2012&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: May">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul>
