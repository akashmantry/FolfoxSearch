<p><b>April 24</b> is the 114th day of the year (115th in <a href="/wiki/Leap_year" title="Leap year">leap years</a>) in the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>. There are 251 days remaining until the end of the year. This date is slightly more likely to fall on a Tuesday, Friday or Sunday (58 in 400 years each) than on Wednesday or Thursday (57), and slightly less likely to occur on a Monday or Saturday (56).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=April_24&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/1479_BC" class="mw-redirect" title="1479 BC">1479 BC</a> – <a href="/wiki/Thutmose_III" title="Thutmose III">Thutmose III</a> ascends to the throne of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">Egypt</a>, although power effectively shifts to <a href="/wiki/Hatshepsut" title="Hatshepsut">Hatshepsut</a> (according to the Low Chronology of the 18th dynasty).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1184_BC" class="mw-redirect" title="1184 BC">1184 BC</a> – Traditional date of the <a href="/wiki/Trojan_War#The_Sack_of_Troy" title="Trojan War">fall of Troy</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1547" title="1547">1547</a> – <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_M%C3%BChlberg" title="Battle of Mühlberg">Battle of Mühlberg</a>. <a href="/wiki/Fernando_%C3%81lvarez_de_Toledo,_3rd_Duke_of_Alba" title="Fernando Álvarez de Toledo, 3rd Duke of Alba">Duke of Alba</a>, commanding Spanish-Imperial forces of <a href="/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles I of Spain</a>, defeats the troops of <a href="/wiki/Schmalkaldic_League" title="Schmalkaldic League">Schmalkaldic League</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1558" title="1558">1558</a> – <a href="/wiki/Mary,_Queen_of_Scots" title="Mary, Queen of Scots">Mary, Queen of Scots</a>, marries the <a href="/wiki/Dauphin_of_France" title="Dauphin of France">Dauphin of France</a>, <a href="/wiki/Francis_II_of_France" title="Francis II of France">François</a>, at <a href="/wiki/Notre_Dame_de_Paris" class="mw-redirect" title="Notre Dame de Paris">Notre Dame de Paris</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1704" title="1704">1704</a> – The first regular newspaper in <a href="/wiki/Thirteen_Colonies" title="Thirteen Colonies">British Colonial America</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boston_News-Letter" title="The Boston News-Letter">The Boston News-Letter</a></i>, is published.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1800" title="1800">1800</a> – The United States <a href="/wiki/Library_of_Congress" title="Library of Congress">Library of Congress</a> is established when President <a href="/wiki/John_Adams" title="John Adams">John Adams</a> signs legislation to appropriate $5,000 to purchase "such books as may be necessary for the use of <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">Congress</a>".</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1877" title="1877">1877</a> – <a href="/wiki/Russo-Turkish_War_(1877%E2%80%9378)" class="mw-redirect" title="Russo-Turkish War (1877–78)">Russo-Turkish War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Russian_Empire" title="Russian Empire">Russian Empire</a> declares war on <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1885" title="1885">1885</a> – American <a href="/wiki/Sharpshooter" title="Sharpshooter">sharpshooter</a> <a href="/wiki/Annie_Oakley" title="Annie Oakley">Annie Oakley</a> is hired by Nate Salsbury to be a part of <a href="/wiki/Buffalo_Bill%27s_Wild_West" class="mw-redirect" title="Buffalo Bill's Wild West">Buffalo Bill's Wild West</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1895" title="1895">1895</a> – <a href="/wiki/Joshua_Slocum" title="Joshua Slocum">Joshua Slocum</a>, the first person to sail single-handedly around the world, sets sail from Boston, Massachusetts aboard the sloop "Spray".</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1913" title="1913">1913</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Woolworth_Building" title="Woolworth Building">Woolworth Building</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Skyscraper" title="Skyscraper">skyscraper</a> in New York City, is opened.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1914" title="1914">1914</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Franck%E2%80%93Hertz_experiment" title="Franck–Hertz experiment">Franck–Hertz experiment</a>, a pillar of <a href="/wiki/Introduction_to_quantum_mechanics" title="Introduction to quantum mechanics">quantum mechanics</a>, is presented to the <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Physikalische_Gesellschaft" title="Deutsche Physikalische Gesellschaft">German Physical Society</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1915" title="1915">1915</a> – The arrest of <a href="/wiki/Deportation_of_Armenian_intellectuals_on_24_April_1915" title="Deportation of Armenian intellectuals on 24 April 1915">250 Armenian intellectuals and community leaders</a> in <a href="/wiki/Istanbul" title="Istanbul">Istanbul</a> marks the beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Genocide" title="Armenian Genocide">Armenian Genocide</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1916" title="1916">1916</a> – <a href="/wiki/Easter_Rising" title="Easter Rising">Easter Rising</a>: Irish rebels, led by <a href="/wiki/Patrick_Pearse" title="Patrick Pearse">Patrick Pearse</a> and <a href="/wiki/James_Connolly" title="James Connolly">James Connolly</a>, launch an uprising in Dublin against British rule and <a href="/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_Irish_Republic" title="Proclamation of the Irish Republic">proclaim an Irish Republic</a>.</li>
<li>1916 – <a href="/wiki/Ernest_Shackleton" title="Ernest Shackleton">Ernest Shackleton</a> and five men of the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Trans-Antarctic_Expedition" title="Imperial Trans-Antarctic Expedition">Imperial Trans-Antarctic Expedition</a> launch a lifeboat from uninhabited <a href="/wiki/Elephant_Island" title="Elephant Island">Elephant Island</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Ocean" title="Southern Ocean">Southern Ocean</a> to organise a rescue for the crew of the sunken <a href="/wiki/Endurance_(1912_ship)" title="Endurance (1912 ship)"><i>Endurance</i></a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1918" title="1918">1918</a> – First tank-to-tank combat, at <a href="/wiki/Villers-Bretonneux" title="Villers-Bretonneux">Villers-Bretonneux</a>, France, when three British <a href="/wiki/Mark_IV_tank" title="Mark IV tank">Mark IVs</a> meet three German <a href="/wiki/A7V" title="A7V">A7Vs</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1922" title="1922">1922</a> – The first segment of the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Wireless_Chain" title="Imperial Wireless Chain">Imperial Wireless Chain</a> providing <a href="/wiki/Wireless_telegraphy" title="Wireless telegraphy">wireless telegraphy</a> between <a href="/wiki/Leafield" title="Leafield">Leafield</a> in <a href="/wiki/Oxfordshire" title="Oxfordshire">Oxfordshire</a>, England, and <a href="/wiki/Cairo" title="Cairo">Cairo</a>, Egypt, comes into operation.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1923" title="1923">1923</a> – In Vienna, the paper <i>Das Ich und das Es</i> (<a href="/wiki/The_Ego_and_the_Id" title="The Ego and the Id">The Ego and the Id</a>) by <a href="/wiki/Sigmund_Freud" title="Sigmund Freud">Sigmund Freud</a> is published, which outlines Freud's theories of <a href="/wiki/Id,_ego_and_super-ego" title="Id, ego and super-ego">the id, ego, and super-ego</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1926" title="1926">1926</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Berlin_(1926)" title="Treaty of Berlin (1926)">Treaty of Berlin</a> is signed. Germany and the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a> each pledge neutrality in the event of an attack on the other by a third party for the next five years.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/1933" title="1933">1933</a> – <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a> begins its <a href="/wiki/Persecution_of_Jehovah%27s_Witnesses_in_Nazi_Germany" title="Persecution of Jehovah's Witnesses in Nazi Germany">persecution of Jehovah's Witnesses</a> by shutting down the <a href="/wiki/Corporations_of_Jehovah%27s_Witnesses" title="Corporations of Jehovah's Witnesses">Watch Tower Society</a> office in <a href="/wiki/Magdeburg" title="Magdeburg">Magdeburg</a>.</li>
