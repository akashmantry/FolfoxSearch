<p><a href="/wiki/Friar" title="Friar">Friar</a> <b>Andrés de Urdaneta, <a href="/wiki/Order_of_St._Augustine" class="mw-redirect" title="Order of St. Augustine">OSA</a></b>, (November 30, 1498 &#160;– June 3, 1568) was a <a href="/wiki/Spaniards" title="Spaniards">Spanish</a> <a href="/wiki/Basques" title="Basques">Basque</a> <a href="/wiki/Circumnavigator" class="mw-redirect" title="Circumnavigator">circumnavigator</a>, explorer and <a href="/wiki/Augustinian_Order" class="mw-redirect" title="Augustinian Order">Augustinian</a> friar. As a navigator he achieved in 1536 the "second" world circumnavigation (after the first one led by <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Magellan" title="Ferdinand Magellan">Ferdinand Magellan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Juan_Sebasti%C3%A1n_Elcano" title="Juan Sebastián Elcano">Juan Sebastián Elcano</a> and their crew in 1522). Urdaneta discovered and plotted a path across <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">the Pacific</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines</a> to <a href="/wiki/Acapulco" title="Acapulco">Acapulco</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Viceroyalty" title="Viceroyalty">Viceroyalty</a> of <a href="/wiki/New_Spain" title="New Spain">New Spain</a> (present day <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>) used by the <a href="/wiki/Manila_galleons" class="mw-redirect" title="Manila galleons">Manila galleons</a>, which came to be known as "Urdaneta's route." He was considered as "protector of the Indians" for his treatment of the <a href="/wiki/Filipino_peoples" class="mw-redirect" title="Filipino peoples">Filipino</a> natives; also <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_Cebu" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Cebu">Cebu</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">Philippines'</a> first <a href="/wiki/Prelate" title="Prelate">prelate</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></p>
<p></p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years">Early years</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9s_de_Urdaneta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early years">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Urdaneta was born in the town of <a href="/wiki/Ordizia" title="Ordizia">Ordizia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a>.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Exploration">Exploration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Andr%C3%A9s_de_Urdaneta&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Exploration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:502px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Andres_Urdaneta_Tornaviaje.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Andres_Urdaneta_Tornaviaje.jpg/500px-Andres_Urdaneta_Tornaviaje.jpg" width="500" height="410" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Andres_Urdaneta_Tornaviaje.jpg/750px-Andres_Urdaneta_Tornaviaje.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Andres_Urdaneta_Tornaviaje.jpg/1000px-Andres_Urdaneta_Tornaviaje.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1593" data-file-height="1306" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">
<div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Andres_Urdaneta_Tornaviaje.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>
Route from Philippines to Acapulco, México</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>Urdaneta was one of the few survivors of the <a href="/wiki/Loa%C3%ADsa_Expedition" class="mw-redirect" title="Loaísa Expedition">Loaísa Expedition</a> to reach the <a href="/wiki/Maluku_Islands" title="Maluku Islands">Spice Islands</a> late in the year 1526, only to be taken prisoner by the Portuguese. Urdaneta spent the next eight and a half years in and around the Spice Islands, but eventually he managed to return to Europe in the <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_India_Armadas" class="mw-redirect" title="Portuguese India Armadas">Portuguese India Armada</a> and under <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">Portuguese</a> guard. Upon his arrival in <a href="/wiki/Lisbon" title="Lisbon">Lisbon</a> on June 26, 1536, he achieved the second world <a href="/wiki/Circumnavigation" title="Circumnavigation">circumnavigation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> Urdaneta accomplished his trip around the world through a journey which lasted just shy of eleven years.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>In Lisbon the Portuguese authorities confiscated his charts and letters. Urdaneta then escaped to Spain, where he recreated much of the confiscated material, and presented it to the Spanish Court. King <a href="/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles I of Spain</a> did not give him a very favourable reception either, and, wearied by his many adventures, he returned to New Spain and there entered the <a href="/wiki/Order_of_Hermits_of_St._Augustine" class="mw-redirect" title="Order of Hermits of St. Augustine">Order of Hermits of St. Augustine</a>.</p>
<p>At the death of the viceroy, <a href="/wiki/Don_(honorific)" title="Don (honorific)">Don</a> <a href="/wiki/Lu%C3%ADs_de_Velasco" title="Luís de Velasco">Luís de Velasco</a>, in 1564, New Spain had passed under the government of the <a href="/wiki/Audiencia_Real" class="mw-redirect" title="Audiencia Real">Audiencia</a>, one of whose first cares was to equip an expedition for the conquest and colonization of the Philippines. This had been ordered by <a href="/wiki/Philip_II_of_Spain" title="Philip II of Spain">Philip II</a> in 1559. Friar Andrés de Urdaneta having been designated as the Commander, the Viceroy had the matter under consideration at the time of his death. Urdaneta was considered a great navigator and especially fitted for cruising in Indian waters. Philip II wrote urging him to join the expedition and offering him the command. Urdaneta agreed to accompany the expedition but refused to take command; the <i>adelantado</i>, Don <a href="/wiki/Miguel_L%C3%B3pez_de_Legazpi" title="Miguel López de Legazpi">Miguel López de Legazpi</a>, was appointed as Commander. The expedition, composed of the <i>Capitana</i>, which carried on board Legazpi and Urdaneta, the <a href="/wiki/Galleon" title="Galleon">galleons</a> <i>San Pablo</i> and <i>San Pedro</i>, and the <a href="/wiki/Ship%27s_tender" title="Ship's tender">tenders</a> <i>San Juan</i> and <i>San Lucas</i>, set sail on November 21, 1564.</p>
<p>Urdaneta founded the first churches in the Philippines, the <a href="/wiki/Cebu_Metropolitan_Cathedral" title="Cebu Metropolitan Cathedral">St. Vitales Church</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Basilica_del_Santo_Ni%C3%B1o" title="Basilica del Santo Niño">Basilica del Santo Niño</a>; he served as the <a href="/wiki/Roman_Catholic_Archdiocese_of_Cebu#Ordinaries" title="Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Cebu">first</a> <a href="/wiki/Prelate" title="Prelate">prelate</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Archdiocese_of_Cebu" class="mw-redirect" title="Archdiocese of Cebu">Church in Cebu</a>. After spending some time in the islands, Legazpi determined to remain and sent Urdaneta back for the purpose of finding a better return route and to obtain help from New Spain for the Philippine colony. (For the problem of sailing east across the Pacific, which Urdaneta solved, see <a href="/wiki/Manila_Galleon" class="mw-redirect" title="Manila Galleon">Manila Galleon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Volta_do_Mar" class="mw-redirect" title="Volta do Mar">Volta do Mar</a>.) Urdaneta set sail from San Miguel (the island of <a href="/wiki/Cebu_Island" class="mw-redirect" title="Cebu Island">Cebu</a>), on June 1, 1565 and was obliged to sail as far as <a href="/wiki/38th_parallel_north" title="38th parallel north">38 degrees North</a> latitude to obtain favourable winds. With the voyage in trouble, Urdaneta had to assume command himself. The ship reached the port of <a href="/wiki/Acapulco" title="Acapulco">Acapulco</a>, on October 8, 1565, having traveled 12,000 miles (20,000&#160;km) in 130 days. Fourteen of the crew had died; only Urdaneta and <a href="/wiki/Felipe_de_Salcedo" title="Felipe de Salcedo">Felipe de Salcedo</a>, nephew of López de Legazpi, had strength enough to cast the anchors.</p>
<p>Upon arriving, Urdaneta discovered that a member of the crew of his expedition, <a href="/wiki/Alonso_de_Arellano" title="Alonso de Arellano">Alonso de Arellano</a>—who had abandoned them just after leaving the port—had actually beaten them across the ocean, arriving at <a href="/wiki/Barra_de_Navidad,_Jalisco" class="mw-redirect" title="Barra de Navidad, Jalisco">Barra de Navidad</a> in <a href="/wiki/Jalisco" title="Jalisco">Jalisco</a> in August of the same year. However, Arellano was in disgrace for his rebellion against the authority of Legazpi, and his notes were far less precise and professional than Urdaneta's, and so the latter's route became the famous and trusted one.</p>

<p>From Mexico, Urdaneta went to Europe to make a report on the expedition and then returned to New Spain, intending to continue on to the Philippines, but he was dissuaded by his friends. He wrote two accounts of his voyages: one giving the account of the Loaiza expedition was published; the other, which gives the account of his return voyage, is preserved in manuscript in the archives of the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_the_Indies" title="Council of the Indies">Council of the Indies</a>.</p>
<p>For the remainder of the 16th and 17th centuries, Spanish ships, particularly the annual <a href="/wiki/Manila_Galleon" class="mw-redirect" title="Manila Galleon">Manila-Acapulco trading Galleon</a>, used "Urdaneta's route."</p>
<p>Urdaneta died in Mexico City in 1568.</p>
