<div id="mp-tfa" style="padding:2px 5px"><b><a href="/wiki/The_Shape_of_Things_to_Come_(Lost)" title="The Shape of Things to Come (Lost)">"The Shape of Things to Come"</a></b> is the 81st <a href="/wiki/List_of_Lost_episodes" title="List of Lost episodes">episode</a> of the <a href="/wiki/American_Broadcasting_Company" title="American Broadcasting Company">American Broadcasting Company</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Lost_(TV_series)" title="Lost (TV series)">Lost</a></i>, and the ninth episode of the <a href="/wiki/Lost_(season_4)" title="Lost (season 4)">fourth season</a>, first aired on April&#160;24, 2008, in the U.S. and Canada. It was written by <a href="/wiki/Drew_Goddard" title="Drew Goddard">Drew Goddard</a> and <a href="/wiki/Brian_K._Vaughan" title="Brian K. Vaughan">Brian K. Vaughan</a> and directed by <a href="/wiki/Jack_Bender" title="Jack Bender">Jack Bender</a>. The narrative centers on <a href="/wiki/Ben_Linus" title="Ben Linus">Ben Linus</a> (played by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Emerson" title="Michael Emerson">Michael Emerson</a>) as he and the <a href="/wiki/Oceanic_Airlines" title="Oceanic Airlines">Oceanic Airlines</a> Flight&#160;815 crash survivors at the Barracks come under attack in December 2004, while <a href="/wiki/Flashforward" title="Flashforward">flashforwards</a> to late 2005 show him recruiting <a href="/wiki/Sayid_Jarrah" title="Sayid Jarrah">Sayid Jarrah</a> (<a href="/wiki/Naveen_Andrews" title="Naveen Andrews">Naveen Andrews</a>) as a <a href="/wiki/Hitman" class="mw-redirect" title="Hitman">hitman</a> and confronting his enemy <a href="/wiki/Charles_Widmore" title="Charles Widmore">Charles Widmore</a> (<a href="/wiki/Alan_Dale" title="Alan Dale">Alan Dale</a>). Production was paused by the 100-day <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%932008_Writers_Guild_of_America_strike" class="mw-redirect" title="2007–2008 Writers Guild of America strike">Writers Guild of America strike</a>. "The Shape of Things to Come" received positive critical reviews and the original broadcast was watched by 14 million Americans. Much praise was directed at Emerson's acting skills, particularly in his reaction to the execution of his character's daughter <a href="/wiki/Alex_(Lost)" class="mw-redirect" title="Alex (Lost)">Alex</a> (<a href="/wiki/Tania_Raymonde" title="Tania Raymonde">Tania Raymonde</a>). His performance in this episode garnered a nomination for <a href="/wiki/Primetime_Emmy_Award_for_Outstanding_Supporting_Actor_-_Drama_Series" class="mw-redirect" title="Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor - Drama Series">Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Drama Series</a> for the <a href="/wiki/60th_Primetime_Emmy_Awards" title="60th Primetime Emmy Awards">60th Primetime Emmy Awards</a>, and the episode was nominated in the category of Outstanding Sound Editing for a Series. (<a href="/wiki/The_Shape_of_Things_to_Come_(Lost)" title="The Shape of Things to Come (Lost)"><b>Full&#160;article...</b></a>)

<div class="thumbinner mp-thumb" style="background: transparent; border: none; padding: 0; max-width: 100px;"><a href="/wiki/File:TuthmosisIII.JPG" class="image" title="Thutmose III"><img alt="Thutmose III" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/TuthmosisIII.JPG/100px-TuthmosisIII.JPG" width="100" height="133" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/TuthmosisIII.JPG/150px-TuthmosisIII.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/TuthmosisIII.JPG/200px-TuthmosisIII.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1704" data-file-height="2272" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption" style="padding: 0.25em 0; word-wrap: break-word;">Thutmose III</div>
</div>
</div>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/1470s_BC" title="1470s BC">1479 BC</a> – <b><a href="/wiki/Thutmose_III" title="Thutmose III">Thutmose III</a></b> <i>(statue pictured)</i> became the sixth <a href="/wiki/Pharaoh" title="Pharaoh">Pharaoh</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Eighteenth_Dynasty_of_Egypt" title="Eighteenth Dynasty of Egypt">Eighteenth Dynasty of Egypt</a>, although during the first 22 years of the reign he was <a href="/wiki/Co-regent" class="mw-redirect" title="Co-regent">co-regent</a> with his aunt, <a href="/wiki/Hatshepsut" title="Hatshepsut">Hatshepsut</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1547" title="1547">1547</a> – <a href="/wiki/Schmalkaldic_War" title="Schmalkaldic War">Schmalkaldic War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor"><span class="nowrap">Charles V,</span> Holy Roman Emperor</a>, led Imperial troops to a decisive victory in the <b><a href="/wiki/Battle_of_M%C3%BChlberg" title="Battle of Mühlberg">Battle of Mühlberg</a></b> over the <a href="/wiki/Lutheranism" title="Lutheranism">Lutheran</a> <a href="/wiki/Schmalkaldic_League" title="Schmalkaldic League">Schmalkaldic League</a> of <a href="/wiki/Protestantism" title="Protestantism">Protestant</a> princes.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1915" title="1915">1915</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Genocide" title="Armenian Genocide">Armenian Genocide</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> began with <b><a href="/wiki/Deportation_of_Armenian_intellectuals_on_24_April_1915" title="Deportation of Armenian intellectuals on 24 April 1915">the arrest and deportation</a></b> of hundreds of prominent Armenians in <a href="/wiki/Constantinople" title="Constantinople">Constantinople</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1933" title="1933">1933</a> – <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Germany" title="Nazi Germany">Nazi Germany</a> began its <b><a href="/wiki/Persecution_of_Jehovah%27s_Witnesses_in_Nazi_Germany" title="Persecution of Jehovah's Witnesses in Nazi Germany">persecution of Jehovah's Witnesses</a></b> by shutting down the <a href="/wiki/Corporations_of_Jehovah%27s_Witnesses" title="Corporations of Jehovah's Witnesses">Watch Tower Society</a> office in <a href="/wiki/Magdeburg" title="Magdeburg">Magdeburg</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1993" title="1993">1993</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Provisional_Irish_Republican_Army" title="Provisional Irish Republican Army">Provisional Irish Republican Army</a> <b><a href="/wiki/1993_Bishopsgate_bombing" title="1993 Bishopsgate bombing">detonated a truck bomb</a></b> in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>'s financial district in <a href="/wiki/Bishopsgate" title="Bishopsgate">Bishopsgate</a>, killing one person, injuring forty-four others, and causing <span class="nowrap">£350 million</span> in damages.</li>
</ul>

<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Cowboys_(AA)_all-time_roster" title="Kansas City Cowboys (AA) all-time roster">Kansas City Cowboys' players</a></b> participated in the <a href="/wiki/American_Association_(19th_century)" title="American Association (19th century)">American Association</a> (AA) for two seasons from 1888 to 1889. A professional baseball team based in <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City,_Missouri" title="Kansas City, Missouri">Kansas City, Missouri</a>, the franchise initially used <a href="/wiki/Association_Park" class="mw-redirect" title="Association Park">Association Park</a> as their home field in 1888, then moved to <a href="/wiki/Exposition_Park_(Kansas_City)" title="Exposition Park (Kansas City)">Exposition Park</a> for the 1889&#160;season. Although the <a href="/wiki/Kansas_City_Cowboys_(American_Association)" title="Kansas City Cowboys (American Association)">Cowboys</a> completed their initial season in last place out of the league's eight teams, there were notable player achievements; on June&#160;6, <a href="/wiki/Henry_Porter_(baseball)" title="Henry Porter (baseball)">Henry Porter</a> threw a <a href="/wiki/No-hitter" title="No-hitter">no-hitter</a>, and on June&#160;13, Barkley hit for the <a href="/wiki/Hitting_for_the_cycle" title="Hitting for the cycle">cycle</a>. The franchise's only future <a href="/wiki/National_Baseball_Hall_of_Fame_and_Museum" title="National Baseball Hall of Fame and Museum">Hall of Fame</a> player, <a href="/wiki/Billy_Hamilton_(baseball,_born_1866)" title="Billy Hamilton (baseball, born 1866)">"Slidin' Billy" Hamilton</a> <i>(pictured)</i>, began his career as a part-time outfielder in 1888, and was their starting <a href="/wiki/Right_fielder" title="Right fielder">right fielder</a> in 1889. He is the franchise's all-time leader in <a href="/wiki/Run_(baseball)" title="Run (baseball)">runs</a> scored, <a href="/wiki/Base_on_balls" title="Base on balls">bases on balls</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Stolen_base" title="Stolen base">stolen bases</a>. With <a href="/wiki/Bill_Watkins" title="Bill Watkins">Bill Watkins</a> as the team's <a href="/wiki/Manager_(baseball)" title="Manager (baseball)">manager</a> in 1889, the team improved to seventh in the league. On November 15, 1889, the Cowboys submitted their resignation from the AA. <td style="padding:0 0.9em 0 0;"><a href="/wiki/File:JesseBJackson.jpg" class="image" title="Jesse B. Jackson"><img alt="Jesse B. <td style="padding:0 6px 0 0">
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Jesse_B._Jackson" title="Jesse B. Jackson">Jesse B. Jackson</a></b> was a United States consul and an <a href="/wiki/Witnesses_and_testimonies_of_the_Armenian_Genocide" title="Witnesses and testimonies of the Armenian Genocide">eyewitness</a> to the <a href="/wiki/Armenian_Genocide" title="Armenian Genocide">Armenian Genocide</a>. He served as consul in <a href="/wiki/Aleppo" title="Aleppo">Aleppo</a> when the town was the junction of many important deportation routes. Jackson concluded that the policies towards the Armenians were carefully planned to "extinguish the Armenian race". He estimated that a million Armenians had been killed and considered his own survival a "miracle". After the genocide, Jackson led a relief effort and was credited with saving the lives of thousands.</p>
