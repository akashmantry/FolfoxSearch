<p><b>1915 (<a href="/wiki/Roman_numerals" title="Roman numerals">MCMXV</a>)</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Common_year" title="Common year">common year</a> <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Friday" title="Common year starting on Friday">starting on Friday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> C) of the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>&#160;and a <a href="/wiki/Common_year_starting_on_Thursday" title="Common year starting on Thursday">common year starting on Thursday</a> (<a href="/wiki/Dominical_letter" title="Dominical letter">dominical letter</a> D) of the <a href="/wiki/Julian_calendar" title="Julian calendar">Julian calendar</a>, the 1915th year of the <a href="/wiki/Common_Era" title="Common Era">Common Era</a> (CE) and <i><a href="/wiki/Anno_Domini" title="Anno Domini">Anno Domini</a></i> (AD) designations, the 915th year of the <a href="/wiki/2nd_millennium" title="2nd millennium">2nd&#160;millennium</a>, the 15th year of the <a href="/wiki/20th_century" title="20th century">20th&#160;century</a>, and the 6th year of the <a href="/wiki/1910s" title="1910s">1910s</a> decade. As of the start of 1915, the Gregorian calendar was 13 days ahead of the Julian calendar, which remained in localized use until 1923.</p>

<p>Below, the events of <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a> have the "WWI" prefix.</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="January">January</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1915&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: January">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/January_1915" title="January 1915">January 1915</a></div>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_1" title="January 1">January 1</a>
<ul>
<li>WWI: The <a href="/wiki/Royal_Navy" title="Royal Navy">Royal Navy</a> battleship <a href="/wiki/HMS_Formidable_(1898)" title="HMS Formidable (1898)">HMS <i>Formidable</i></a> is sunk off <a href="/wiki/Lyme_Regis" title="Lyme Regis">Lyme Regis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dorset" title="Dorset">Dorset</a>, England, by an <a href="/wiki/Imperial_German_Navy" title="Imperial German Navy">Imperial German Navy</a> <a href="/wiki/U-boat" title="U-boat">U-boat</a> with the loss of 547 crew.</li>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Broken_Hill" title="Battle of Broken Hill">Battle of Broken Hill</a>, a train ambush near <a href="/wiki/Broken_Hill,_New_South_Wales" class="mw-redirect" title="Broken Hill, New South Wales">Broken Hill, New South Wales</a>, in Australia, is carried out by two men (claiming to be in support of the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>) who are killed together with 4 civilians.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Harry_Houdini" title="Harry Houdini">Harry Houdini</a> performs a <a href="/wiki/Straitjacket" title="Straitjacket">straitjacket</a> escape performance.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup></li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_5" title="January 5">January 5</a> – <a href="/wiki/Joseph_E._Carberry" title="Joseph E. Carberry">Joseph E. Carberry</a> sets an altitude record of 11,690 feet (3,560 m), carrying Capt. Benjamin Delahauf Foulois as a passenger in a fixed-wing aircraft.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_12" title="January 12">January 12</a>
<ul>
<li>The <a href="/wiki/United_States_House_of_Representatives" title="United States House of Representatives">United States House of Representatives</a> rejects a proposal to give women the right to vote.</li>
<li><i><a href="/wiki/A_Fool_There_Was_(1915_film)" title="A Fool There Was (1915 film)">A Fool There Was</a></i> premières in the United States starring <a href="/wiki/Theda_Bara" title="Theda Bara">Theda Bara</a> as a <i><a href="/wiki/Femme_fatale" title="Femme fatale">femme fatale</a></i>; she quickly becomes one of early cinema's most sensational stars.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_13" title="January 13">January 13</a> – The 6.7 <a href="/wiki/Moment_magnitude_scale" title="Moment magnitude scale">Mw</a> <a href="/wiki/1915_Avezzano_earthquake" title="1915 Avezzano earthquake">Avezzano earthquake</a> shakes the <a href="/wiki/Province_of_L%27Aquila" title="Province of L'Aquila">Province of L'Aquila</a> in Italy with a maximum <a href="/wiki/Mercalli_intensity_scale" title="Mercalli intensity scale">Mercalli intensity</a> of XI (<i>Extreme</i>). Various agencies estimate the number of people killed to be 29,978–32,610.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_18" title="January 18">January 18</a> – <a href="/wiki/Twenty-One_Demands" title="Twenty-One Demands">Twenty-One Demands</a> from <a href="/wiki/Empire_of_Japan" title="Empire of Japan">Japan</a> to <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China_(1912%E2%80%9349)" title="Republic of China (1912–49)">China</a> are made.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_19" title="January 19">January 19</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Georges_Claude" title="Georges Claude">Georges Claude</a> patents the <a href="/wiki/Neon_discharge_tube" class="mw-redirect" title="Neon discharge tube">neon discharge tube</a> for use in advertising.</li>
<li>WWI: German <a href="/wiki/Zeppelin" title="Zeppelin">Zeppelins</a> bomb the coastal towns of <a href="/wiki/Great_Yarmouth" title="Great Yarmouth">Great Yarmouth</a> and <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Lynn" title="King's Lynn">King's Lynn</a> in England for the first time, killing more than 20.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_21" title="January 21">January 21</a> – <a href="/wiki/Kiwanis" title="Kiwanis">Kiwanis</a> is founded in <a href="/wiki/Detroit" title="Detroit">Detroit</a>, as The Supreme Lodge Benevolent Order Brothers.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_24" title="January 24">January 24</a> – WW I: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Dogger_Bank_(1915)" title="Battle of Dogger Bank (1915)">Battle of Dogger Bank</a>: <a href="/wiki/British_Grand_Fleet" class="mw-redirect" title="British Grand Fleet">British Grand Fleet</a> defeats the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_German_Navy" title="Imperial German Navy">German High Seas Fleet</a>, sinking the armoured cruiser <a href="/wiki/SMS_Bl%C3%BCcher" title="SMS Blücher">SMS&#160;<i>Blücher</i></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Pocket_On_This_Day_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Pocket_On_This_Day-3">[3]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_25" title="January 25">January 25</a>
<ul>
<li>First United States coast-to-coast <a href="/wiki/Long-distance_calling" title="Long-distance calling">long-distance telephone call</a>, facilitated by a newly invented vacuum tube amplifier, ceremonially inaugurated by <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Graham_Bell" title="Alexander Graham Bell">Alexander Graham Bell</a> in New York City and his former assistant <a href="/wiki/Thomas_A._Watson" title="Thomas A. Watson">Thomas A. Watson</a>, in San Francisco, California.</li>
<li>Emory College is rechartered as <a href="/wiki/Emory_University" title="Emory University">Emory University</a>, and plans to move its main campus from <a href="/wiki/Oxford,_Georgia" title="Oxford, Georgia">Oxford, Georgia</a> to <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_26" title="January 26">January 26</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Mountain_National_Park" title="Rocky Mountain National Park">Rocky Mountain National Park</a> is established by an act of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">United States Congress</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_26" title="January 26">January 26</a> – WWI: <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Army" class="mw-redirect" title="Ottoman Army">Ottoman Army</a> begins the <a href="/wiki/Raid_on_the_Suez_Canal" title="Raid on the Suez Canal">Raid on the Suez Canal</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_27" title="January 27">January 27</a> – WWI: Military casualties begin arriving at the <a href="/wiki/H%C3%B4pital_Temporaire_d%27Arc-en-Barrois" title="Hôpital Temporaire d'Arc-en-Barrois">Hôpital Temporaire d'Arc-en-Barrois</a>, established earlier in the month.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_28" title="January 28">January 28</a> – An act of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">United States Congress</a> designates the <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">United States Coast Guard</a>, began in 1790, as a military branch.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/January_31" title="January 31">January 31</a> – WWI: <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">Germany</a>'s first large-scale use of <a href="/wiki/Chemical_weapons_in_World_War_I" title="Chemical weapons in World War I">poison gas</a> as a weapon occurs when 18,000 <a href="/wiki/Artillery" title="Artillery">artillery</a> shells containing liquid <a href="/wiki/Xylyl_bromide" title="Xylyl bromide">xylyl bromide</a> <a href="/wiki/Tear_gas" title="Tear gas">tear gas</a> are fired on the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Russian_Army" title="Imperial Russian Army">Imperial Russian Army</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Rawka_River" class="mw-redirect" title="Rawka River">Rawka River</a> west of <a href="/wiki/Warsaw" title="Warsaw">Warsaw</a> during the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Bolim%C3%B3w" title="Battle of Bolimów">Battle of Bolimów</a>; however, freezing temperatures prevent it being effective.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="February">February</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1915&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: February">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/February_1915" title="February 1915">February 1915</a></div>
<ul>
<li>February – While working as a <a href="/wiki/Cook_(profession)" title="Cook (profession)">cook</a> at New York's <a href="/wiki/Sloane_Hospital_for_Women" title="Sloane Hospital for Women">Sloane Hospital for Women</a> under an assumed name, "<a href="/wiki/Typhoid_Mary" class="mw-redirect" title="Typhoid Mary">Typhoid Mary</a>" (an <a href="/wiki/Asymptomatic_carrier" title="Asymptomatic carrier">asymptomatic carrier</a> of <a href="/wiki/Typhoid_fever" title="Typhoid fever">typhoid fever</a>) infects 25 people, and is placed in <a href="/wiki/Quarantine" title="Quarantine">quarantine</a> for life on <a href="/wiki/March_27" title="March 27">March 27</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_4" title="February 4">February 4</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Maritz_Rebellion" class="mw-redirect" title="Maritz Rebellion">Maritz Rebellion</a> of disaffected <a href="/wiki/Boer" title="Boer">Boere</a> against the government of the <a href="/wiki/Union_of_South_Africa" title="Union of South Africa">Union of South Africa</a> ends with the surrender of remaining rebels.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_8" title="February 8">February 8</a> – The controversial film, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Birth_of_a_Nation" title="The Birth of a Nation">The Birth of a Nation</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/D._W._Griffith" title="D. W. Griffith">D. W. Griffith</a>, premieres in Los Angeles. It will be the <a href="/wiki/List_of_highest-grossing_films#Timeline_of_highest-grossing_films" title="List of highest-grossing films">highest-grossing film</a> for around 25 years.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_18" title="February 18">February 18</a> – WWI: Germany regards waters around the British Isles to be a war zone from this date, as part of its <a href="/wiki/U-boat_Campaign_(World_War_I)" title="U-boat Campaign (World War I)">U-boat campaign</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/February_20" title="February 20">February 20</a> – In San Francisco the <a href="/wiki/Panama%E2%80%93Pacific_International_Exposition" title="Panama–Pacific International Exposition">Panama–Pacific International Exposition</a> is opened.</li>
</ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="March">March</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1915&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: March">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/March_1915" title="March 1915">March 1915</a></div>
<ul>
<li>March – The <a href="/wiki/1915_Palestine_locust_infestation" title="1915 Palestine locust infestation">1915 Palestine locust infestation</a> breaks out in <a href="/wiki/Palestine_(region)" title="Palestine (region)">Palestine</a>; it continues until October.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_3" title="March 3">March 3</a> – The <a href="/wiki/National_Advisory_Committee_for_Aeronautics" title="National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics">National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics</a>, the predecessor of <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a>, is founded in the United States.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_10" title="March 10">March 10</a>–<a href="/wiki/March_13" title="March 13">13</a> – WWI: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Neuve_Chapelle" title="Battle of Neuve Chapelle">Battle of Neuve Chapelle</a>, the first deliberately planned British offensive of the War: British Indian troops overrun German positions in France, but are unable to sustain the advance.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_11" title="March 11">March 11</a> – WWI: British <a href="/wiki/Armed_merchantman" title="Armed merchantman">armed merchantman</a> <a href="/wiki/HMS_Bayano_(1913)" title="HMS Bayano (1913)">HMS&#160;<i>Bayano</i>&#160;(1913)</a> is sunk in the <a href="/wiki/North_Channel_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="North Channel (Great Britain and Ireland)">North Channel</a> off the coast of <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> by <a href="/wiki/Imperial_German_Navy" title="Imperial German Navy">Imperial German Navy</a> <a href="/wiki/U-boat" title="U-boat">U-boat</a> <i><a href="/wiki/SM_U-27_(Germany)" title="SM U-27 (Germany)">SM U-27</a></i>. Around 200 crew are lost, a number of bodies being washed up on the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a>, with only 26 saved.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/March_14" title="March 14">March 14</a> – WWI:
<ul>

Willard becomes very popular among white Americans for "bringing back the championship to the white race".</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/April_25" title="April 25">April 25</a> – WWI: Start of the <a href="/wiki/Gallipoli_Campaign" title="Gallipoli Campaign">Gallipoli Campaign</a> (lasting until January 1916): <a href="/wiki/Landing_at_Anzac_Cove" title="Landing at Anzac Cove">Landing at Anzac Cove</a> by <a href="/wiki/Australian_and_New_Zealand_Army_Corps" title="Australian and New Zealand Army Corps">Australian and New Zealand Army Corps</a> and <a href="/wiki/Landing_at_Cape_Helles" title="Landing at Cape Helles">landing at Cape Helles</a> by British and French troops to begin the Allied invasion of the <a href="/wiki/Gallipoli" title="Gallipoli">Gallipoli</a> peninsula in the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a>.</li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="May">May</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1915&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: May">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/May_1915" title="May 1915">May 1915</a></div>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_1" title="May 1">May 1</a> – WWI: General <a href="/wiki/Louis_Botha" title="Louis Botha">Louis Botha</a>, <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_South_Africa" title="Prime Minister of South Africa">Prime Minister of South Africa</a>, leads the army in the occupation of <a href="/wiki/German_South_West_Africa" title="German South West Africa">German South West Africa</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_3" title="May 3">May 3</a> – Canadian soldier <a href="/wiki/John_McCrae" title="John McCrae">John McCrae</a> writes the poem "<a href="/wiki/In_Flanders_Fields" title="In Flanders Fields">In Flanders Fields</a>".</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_5" title="May 5">May 5</a> – WWI: Forces of the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> begin shelling <a href="/wiki/ANZAC_Cove" title="ANZAC Cove">ANZAC Cove</a> from a new position behind their lines.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_6" title="May 6">May 6</a> – Baseball player <a href="/wiki/Babe_Ruth" title="Babe Ruth">Babe Ruth</a> hits his first career home run (off <a href="/wiki/Jack_Warhop" title="Jack Warhop">Jack Warhop</a>), for the <a href="/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox" title="Boston Red Sox">Boston Red Sox</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_7" title="May 7">May 7</a> – WWI: <a href="/wiki/Sinking_of_the_RMS_Lusitania" title="Sinking of the RMS Lusitania">Sinking of the RMS <i>Lusitania</i></a>: British <a href="/wiki/Ocean_liner" title="Ocean liner">ocean liner</a> <a href="/wiki/RMS_Lusitania" title="RMS Lusitania">RMS&#160;<i>Lusitania</i></a> is sunk by <a href="/wiki/Imperial_German_Navy" title="Imperial German Navy">Imperial German Navy</a> <a href="/wiki/U-boat" title="U-boat">U-boat</a> <i><a href="/wiki/SM_U-20_(Germany)" title="SM U-20 (Germany)">U-20</a></i> off the south-west coast of Ireland, killing 1,198 civilians en route from New York City to <a href="/wiki/Liverpool" title="Liverpool">Liverpool</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_9" title="May 9">May 9</a> – WWI: <a href="/wiki/Second_Battle_of_Artois" title="Second Battle of Artois">Second Battle of Artois</a>: <a href="/wiki/German_Empire" title="German Empire">German</a> and <a href="/wiki/French_Third_Republic" title="French Third Republic">French</a> forces fight to a standstill; German forces defeat the British at the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Aubers_Ridge" title="Battle of Aubers Ridge">Battle of Aubers Ridge</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_17" title="May 17">May 17</a> – The last purely <a href="/wiki/Liberal_Party_(UK)" title="Liberal Party (UK)">Liberal</a> government in the United Kingdom ends when the prime minister <a href="/wiki/H._H._Asquith" title="H. H. Asquith">H. H. Asquith</a> forms an all-party <a href="/wiki/Coalition_government" title="Coalition government">coalition government</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Second_Asquith_ministry" title="Second Asquith ministry">Second Asquith ministry</a>, with effect from May 25.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_19" title="May 19">May 19</a> – WWI: <a href="/wiki/Third_attack_on_Anzac_Cove" title="Third attack on Anzac Cove">Third attack on Anzac Cove</a> by Ottoman forces is repelled by the <a href="/wiki/Australian_and_New_Zealand_Army_Corps" title="Australian and New Zealand Army Corps">Australian and New Zealand Army Corps</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_22" title="May 22">May 22</a>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/Quintinshill_rail_disaster" title="Quintinshill rail disaster">Quintinshill rail disaster</a> in Scotland: collision and fire kill 226, mostly troops, the largest number of fatalities in a <a href="/wiki/List_of_rail_accidents_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="List of rail accidents in the United Kingdom">rail accident in the United Kingdom</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Lassen_Peak" title="Lassen Peak">Lassen Peak</a>, one of the <a href="/wiki/Cascade_Volcanoes" title="Cascade Volcanoes">Cascade Volcanoes</a> in <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, erupts, sending an ash plume 30,000 feet in the air and devastating the nearby area with <a href="/wiki/Pyroclastic_flow" title="Pyroclastic flow">pyroclastic flows</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lahar" title="Lahar">lahars</a>. It is the last volcano to erupt in the contiguous United States until the <a href="/wiki/1980_eruption_of_Mount_St._Helens" title="1980 eruption of Mount St. Helens">1980 eruption of Mount St. Helens</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_24" title="May 24">May 24</a> – WWI: <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy" title="Kingdom of Italy">Italy</a> joins the <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_I" title="Allies of World War I">Allies</a> after they declare war on <a href="/wiki/Austria-Hungary" title="Austria-Hungary">Austria-Hungary</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_25" title="May 25">May 25</a> – <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China_(1912%E2%80%9349)" title="Republic of China (1912–49)">China</a> agrees to the <a href="/wiki/Twenty-One_Demands" title="Twenty-One Demands">Twenty-One Demands</a> of the Japanese.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/May_29" title="May 29">May 29</a> – <a href="/wiki/Te%C3%B3filo_Braga" title="Teófilo Braga">Teófilo Braga</a> becomes president of <a href="/wiki/First_Portuguese_Republic" title="First Portuguese Republic">Portugal</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="July">July</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1915&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: July">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

South Africa will occupy <a href="/wiki/South_West_Africa" title="South West Africa">South West Africa</a> until March <a href="/wiki/1990" title="1990">1990</a>.</li>

<ul>
<li>An extremely overloaded <a href="/wiki/International_Railway_(New_York%E2%80%93Ontario)" title="International Railway (New York–Ontario)">International Railway (New York–Ontario)</a> <a href="/wiki/Tram" title="Tram">trolleycar</a> with 157 passengers crashes near <a href="/wiki/Queenston,_Ontario" title="Queenston, Ontario">Queenston, Ontario</a>, resulting in 15 casualties.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Sinhalese_people" title="Sinhalese people">Sinhalese</a> militia captain <a href="/wiki/Henry_Pedris" title="Henry Pedris">Henry Pedris</a> is executed in <a href="/wiki/British_Ceylon" title="British Ceylon">British Ceylon</a> for inciting race riots, a charge later proved false; he becomes a hero of the <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lankan_independence_movement" title="Sri Lankan independence movement">Sri Lankan independence movement</a>.</li>
</ul>
</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/July_11" title="July 11">July 11</a> – WWI: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Rufiji_Delta" title="Battle of Rufiji Delta">Battle of Rufiji Delta</a> – German cruiser <a href="/wiki/SMS_K%C3%B6nigsberg_(1905)" title="SMS Königsberg (1905)">SMS&#160;<i>Königsberg</i>&#160;(1905)</a> is forced to <a href="/wiki/Scuttling" title="Scuttling">scuttle</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Rufiji_River" title="Rufiji River">Rufiji River</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_14" title="July 14">July 14</a> – The <a href="/wiki/McMahon%E2%80%93Hussein_Correspondence" title="McMahon–Hussein Correspondence">McMahon–Hussein Correspondence</a> between <a href="/wiki/Hussein_bin_Ali,_Sharif_of_Mecca" title="Hussein bin Ali, Sharif of Mecca">Hussein bin Ali, Sharif of Mecca</a> and the British oficial <a href="/wiki/Henry_McMahon_(diplomat)" class="mw-redirect" title="Henry McMahon (diplomat)">Henry McMahon</a> concerning the <a href="/wiki/Arab_revolt" class="mw-redirect" title="Arab revolt">Arab revolt</a> against the <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman Empire</a> begins.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_22" title="July 22">July 22</a> – WWI: "<a href="/wiki/Great_Retreat_(Russian)" title="Great Retreat (Russian)">The Great Retreat</a>" is ordered on Eastern front – Russian forces pull back out of Poland (then part of Russia), taking machinery and equipment with them.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_24" title="July 24">July 24</a> – The steamer <a href="/wiki/SS_Eastland" title="SS Eastland"><i>Eastland</i></a> capsizes in central Chicago, with the loss of 844 lives.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/July_28" title="July 28">July 28</a> – The <a href="/wiki/American_occupation_of_Haiti" class="mw-redirect" title="American occupation of Haiti">American occupation of Haiti</a> (1915–34) begins.</li>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="September">September</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=1915&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: September">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/September_1915" title="September 1915">September 1915</a></div>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_5" title="September 5">September 5</a> – The beginning of the <a href="/wiki/Zimmerwald_Conference" title="Zimmerwald Conference">Zimmerwald Conference</a> in <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_6" title="September 6">September 6</a> – The prototype <a href="/wiki/Tanks_in_World_War_I" title="Tanks in World War I">military tank</a> is first tested by the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_7" title="September 7">September 7</a> – Former cartoonist <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Gruelle" title="Johnny Gruelle">John B. Gruelle</a> is given a patent for his <i><a href="/wiki/Raggedy_Ann" title="Raggedy Ann">Raggedy Ann</a></i> doll.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_8" title="September 8">September 8</a> – WWI: A <a href="/wiki/Zeppelin" title="Zeppelin">Zeppelin</a> raid destroys No.61 <a href="/wiki/Farringdon_Road" title="Farringdon Road">Farringdon Road</a>, London. It Is rebuilt in <a href="/wiki/1917" title="1917">1917</a> and called The Zeppelin Building.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_11" title="September 11">September 11</a> – The <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_Railroad" title="Pennsylvania Railroad">Pennsylvania Railroad</a> begins electrified commuter rail service between <a href="/wiki/Paoli,_Pennsylvania" title="Paoli, Pennsylvania">Paoli</a> and <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a>, using overhead AC trolley wires for power. This type of system is later used in long-distance passenger trains between New York City, Washington, D.C., and <a href="/wiki/Harrisburg,_Pennsylvania" title="Harrisburg, Pennsylvania">Harrisburg, Pennsylvania</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_25" title="September 25">September 25</a>–<a href="/wiki/October_14" title="October 14">October 14</a> – WWI: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Loos" title="Battle of Loos">Battle of Loos</a>: British forces take the <a href="/wiki/French_Third_Republic" title="French Third Republic">French</a> town of <a href="/wiki/Loos-en-Gohelle" title="Loos-en-Gohelle">Loos</a> but with substantial casualties and are unable to press their advantage. This is the first time the British use <a href="/wiki/Poison_gas_in_World_War_I" class="mw-redirect" title="Poison gas in World War I">poison gas in World War I</a> and also their first large-scale use of 'New' or <a href="/wiki/Kitchener%27s_Army" title="Kitchener's Army">Kitchener's Army</a> units.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/September_30" title="September 30">September 30</a> – WWI: <a href="/wiki/Serbian_Army" title="Serbian Army">Serbian Army</a> <a href="/wiki/Private_(rank)" title="Private (rank)">private</a> <a href="/wiki/Radoje_Ljutovac" title="Radoje Ljutovac">Radoje Ljutovac</a> became the first soldier in history to shoot down an enemy aircraft with <a href="/wiki/Anti-aircraft_warfare" title="Anti-aircraft warfare">ground-to-air</a> fire.</li>
</ul>

Historian General of the UDC, <a href="/wiki/Mildred_Rutherford" class="mw-redirect" title="Mildred Rutherford">Mildred Rutherford</a> address the gathering on the "Historical Sins of Omission &amp; Commission" of <a href="/wiki/Yankee" title="Yankee">Yankee</a> historians.</li>
