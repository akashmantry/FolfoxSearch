<p><b>August 14</b> is the 226th day of the year (227th in <a href="/wiki/Leap_year" title="Leap year">leap years</a>) in the <a href="/wiki/Gregorian_calendar" title="Gregorian calendar">Gregorian calendar</a>. There are 139 days remaining until the end of the year. This date is slightly more likely to fall on a Tuesday, Friday or Sunday (58 in 400 years each) than on Wednesday or Thursday (57), and slightly less likely to occur on a Monday or Saturday (56).</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=August_14&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul>
<li><a href="/wiki/29_BC" title="29 BC">29 BC</a> – <a href="/wiki/Octavian" class="mw-redirect" title="Octavian">Octavian</a> holds the second of three consecutive <a href="/wiki/Roman_triumph" title="Roman triumph">triumphs</a> in <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Rome" title="Ancient Rome">Rome</a> to celebrate the victory over the <a href="/wiki/Dalmatian_tribes" class="mw-redirect" title="Dalmatian tribes">Dalmatian tribes</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1040" title="1040">1040</a> – King <a href="/wiki/Duncan_I_of_Scotland" title="Duncan I of Scotland">Duncan I</a> is killed in battle against his first cousin and rival <a href="/wiki/Macbeth,_King_of_Scotland" title="Macbeth, King of Scotland">Macbeth</a>. The latter succeeds him as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Scottish_monarchs" title="List of Scottish monarchs">King of Scotland</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1183" title="1183">1183</a> – <a href="/wiki/Taira_no_Munemori" title="Taira no Munemori">Taira no Munemori</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Taira_clan" title="Taira clan">Taira clan</a> take the young <a href="/wiki/Emperor_Antoku" title="Emperor Antoku">Emperor Antoku</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Imperial_Regalia_of_Japan" title="Imperial Regalia of Japan">three sacred treasures</a> and flee to western Japan to escape pursuit by the <a href="/wiki/Minamoto_clan" title="Minamoto clan">Minamoto clan</a> (traditional <a href="/wiki/Japanese_calendar" title="Japanese calendar">Japanese date</a>: Twenty-fifth Day of the Seventh Month of the Second Year of <a href="/wiki/Juei" title="Juei">Juei</a>).</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1288" title="1288">1288</a> – Count <a href="/wiki/Adolf_VIII_of_Berg" title="Adolf VIII of Berg">Adolf VIII of Berg</a> grants <a href="/wiki/Town_privileges" title="Town privileges">town privileges</a> to <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCsseldorf" title="Düsseldorf">Düsseldorf</a>, the village on the banks of the <a href="/wiki/D%C3%BCssel" title="Düssel">Düssel</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1352" title="1352">1352</a> – <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Breton_Succession" title="War of the Breton Succession">War of the Breton Succession</a>: Anglo-Bretons defeat the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_France" title="Kingdom of France">French</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Mauron" title="Battle of Mauron">Battle of Mauron</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1370" title="1370">1370</a> – <a href="/wiki/Charles_IV,_Holy_Roman_Emperor" title="Charles IV, Holy Roman Emperor">Charles IV, Holy Roman Emperor</a>, grants <a href="/wiki/City_privileges" class="mw-redirect" title="City privileges">city privileges</a> to <a href="/wiki/Karlovy_Vary" title="Karlovy Vary">Carlsbad</a> which is subsequently named after him.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1385" title="1385">1385</a> – <a href="/wiki/1383%E2%80%9385_Portuguese_interregnum" class="mw-redirect" title="1383–85 Portuguese interregnum">Portuguese Crisis of 1383–85</a>: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Aljubarrota" title="Battle of Aljubarrota">Battle of Aljubarrota</a>: Portuguese forces commanded by <a href="/wiki/John_I_of_Portugal" title="John I of Portugal">King John I</a> and his <a href="/wiki/General_officer" title="General officer">general</a> <a href="/wiki/Nuno_%C3%81lvares_Pereira" title="Nuno Álvares Pereira">Nuno Álvares Pereira</a> defeat the <a href="/wiki/Castile_(historical_region)" title="Castile (historical region)">Castilian</a> army of <a href="/wiki/John_I_of_Castile" title="John I of Castile">King John I</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1480" title="1480">1480</a> – <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_invasion_of_Otranto" title="Ottoman invasion of Otranto">Battle of Otranto</a>: <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">Ottoman</a> troops behead 800 <a href="/wiki/Christian" title="Christian">Christians</a> for refusing to convert to <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islam</a>; they are later <a href="/wiki/Martyrs_of_Otranto" title="Martyrs of Otranto">honored</a> in the Church.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1598" title="1598">1598</a> – <a href="/wiki/Nine_Years%27_War_(Ireland)" title="Nine Years' War (Ireland)">Nine Years' War</a>: <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Yellow_Ford" title="Battle of the Yellow Ford">Battle of the Yellow Ford</a>: Irish forces under <a href="/wiki/Hugh_O%27Neill,_Earl_of_Tyrone" title="Hugh O'Neill, Earl of Tyrone">Hugh O'Neill, Earl of Tyrone</a>, defeats an English expeditionary force under <a href="/wiki/Henry_Bagenal" title="Henry Bagenal">Henry Bagenal</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1720" title="1720">1720</a> – The Spanish military <a href="/wiki/Villasur_expedition" title="Villasur expedition">Villasur expedition</a> is wiped out by <a href="/wiki/Pawnee_people" title="Pawnee people">Pawnee</a> and <a href="/wiki/Otoe_tribe" class="mw-redirect" title="Otoe tribe">Otoe</a> warriors near present-day <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Nebraska" title="Columbus, Nebraska">Columbus, Nebraska</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1816" title="1816">1816</a> – The United Kingdom formally annexes the <a href="/wiki/Tristan_da_Cunha" title="Tristan da Cunha">Tristan da Cunha</a> archipelago, administering the islands from the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Colony" title="Cape Colony">Cape Colony</a> in South Africa.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1842" title="1842">1842</a> – <a href="/wiki/American_Indian_Wars" title="American Indian Wars">American Indian Wars</a>: <a href="/wiki/Seminole_Wars" title="Seminole Wars">Second Seminole War</a> ends, with the <a href="/wiki/Seminole" title="Seminole">Seminoles</a> forced from <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> to <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">Oklahoma</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1848" title="1848">1848</a> – <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Territory" title="Oregon Territory">Oregon Territory</a> is organized by <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Congress" title="Act of Congress">act of Congress</a>.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1880" title="1880">1880</a> – Construction of <a href="/wiki/Cologne_Cathedral" title="Cologne Cathedral">Cologne Cathedral</a>, the most famous landmark in <a href="/wiki/Cologne" title="Cologne">Cologne</a>, Germany, is completed.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1885" title="1885">1885</a> – <a href="/wiki/Japanese_patent_law" title="Japanese patent law">Japan's first patent</a> is issued to the inventor of a rust-proof paint.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1888" title="1888">1888</a> – An <a href="/wiki/Sound_recording_and_reproduction" title="Sound recording and reproduction">audio recording</a> of English composer <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Sullivan" title="Arthur Sullivan">Arthur Sullivan</a>'s "<a href="/wiki/The_Lost_Chord" title="The Lost Chord">The Lost Chord</a>", one of the first recordings of music ever made, is played during a press conference introducing <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Edison" title="Thomas Edison">Thomas Edison</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Phonograph" title="Phonograph">phonograph</a> in London, England.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/1893" title="1893">1893</a> – France becomes the first country to introduce <a href="/wiki/Vehicle_registration_plate" title="Vehicle registration plate">motor vehicle registration</a>.</li>

<li><a href="/wiki/1901" title="1901">1901</a> – The first claimed <a href="/wiki/Aviation" title="Aviation">powered flight</a>, by <a href="/wiki/Gustave_Whitehead" title="Gustave Whitehead">Gustave Whitehead</a> in his <a href="/wiki/Whitehead_No._21" title="Whitehead No. 21">Number 21</a>.</li>
